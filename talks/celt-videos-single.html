Using videos in mathematics teaching

Lots already on the web ...

  • Mathcentre
  • MIT OpenCourseWare
  • YouTube: MathDoctorBob, N J Wildberger, ...
  • ...

... so why make them myself?

  • tailor them to my topics and level,
  • a first port of call when faced with repeated questions on basic techniques, problems, ...

Example - Dr Killian O'Brien, SCMDT

  • A divisibility proof using induction
  • Ok, fairly good, but ...
  • there is much missing:
    • detailed explanations,
    • emphasis on what's important and what's less so,
    • thought processes that lead me to this solution.

Example - Dr Killian O'Brien, SCMDT

  • Seeing and hearing the solution develop can supply these missing details

Example - Prof. Clive Mingham, SCMDT

  • Also good for code
  • The text of a program misses similar details
  • which are filled in by seeing and hearing the code develop

Example, Dr Jon Shiach, SCMDT

Some quotes from the research

Radical change to HE practice?

Examining the students responses to this relatively new technology raises questions on the viability of the traditional face to face lecture and the role academics will play in a technology driven tertiary sector.

-- Mullamphy, D., Higgins, P., Belward, S., & Ward, L. (2010). To screencast or not to screencast. ANZIAM Journal, 51, C446--C460. doi:10.0000/anziamj.v51i0.2657

Overheads?

I estimate that the amount of my own time that I need to dedicate to processing all of the materials and making them available on the web now comes to between twenty and thirty minutes per class.

-- Using a tablet PC and screencasts when teaching mathematics to undergraduates, case study, from the MoreMathsGrads collection Maths at University: reflections on experience, practice and provision, editors Mike Robinson, Neil Challis and Mike Thomlinson (2010)
