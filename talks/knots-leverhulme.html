Background

  • PhD in Algebraic Topology with applications to knot theory

Previous work

  • My PhD research concerned Grobner basis type algorithms for handling algebraic objects (ideals) in the ring of (Laurent) polynomials with integer coefficients.

Connection with knot theory

  • One aim of mathematical knot theory is to develop tools (invariants) that help to classify the different knot types, for instance to produce the table of prime knots
  • Certain invariants of knots (the Alexander ideals) require these algorithms to be able to discriminate between different ideals and so use the invariants to help classify knots.
